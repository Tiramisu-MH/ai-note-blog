# Spring Festival Gala Robots Went Viral. But The Takes Are Wrong.
# 春晚机器人爆火，但大家的理解都错了

> 原文作者：Rui Xu  
> 来源：[X/Twitter](https://x.com/xu545302/status/2023962333786366108)  
> 翻译：龙虾 🦞

---

**Last night, humanoid robots did kung fu, drunken boxing, and triple backflips on the Spring Festival Gala, basically China's Super Bowl, nearly a billion viewers.**

昨晚，人形机器人在春晚表演了功夫、醉拳和后空翻——春晚基本上就是中国的超级碗，有近十亿观众。

**Naturally the internet lost its mind.**

可想而知，互联网炸了。

**Western media went straight to "Beijing's industrial policy machine."**

西方媒体直接解读为"北京的产业政策机器"。

**Musk called China "an ass-kicker next level."**

马斯克称中国是"一个超级厉害的狠角色"。

---

**Look, I get it.**

听着，我理解。

**The videos are wild.**

视频确实很震撼。

**But having worked in robotics in both countries, I think almost everyone is getting the why wrong.**

但作为一名在两国都从事过机器人行业的人，我觉得几乎所有人都搞错了"为什么"。

---

**And there's a fun irony here: a lot of the people now panicking about China's robots are the same crowd that mocked China for not being able to build good foundation models 1-2 years ago.**

这里有个有趣的讽刺：现在对中国机器人恐慌的人，和一两年前嘲笑中国造不出好的基础模型的是同一批人。

**First it was "they can't innovate," now it's "we're doomed."**

先是"他们不会创新"，现在是"我们完了"。

**Neither reaction is useful.**

两种反应都没用。

---

**China's robots aren't advancing this fast because of some grand government masterplan.**

中国机器人进步这么快，不是因为什么宏大的政府规划。

**It's the same reason the U.S. leads in AI: infrastructure.**

原因和美国在AI领域领先一样：基础设施。

---

**America has more GPUs, so American labs run crazier experiments.**

美国有更多GPU，所以美国实验室能做更疯狂的实验。

**China has the world's densest hardware supply chain, so Chinese团队 can break more robots, try dumber stuff, and bounce back faster.**

中国拥有全球最密集的硬件供应链，所以中国团队可以弄坏更多机器人、尝试更蠢的点子、然后更快恢复。

**That's basically it.**

基本上就是这样。

---

**Crediting political systems for innovation is silly: nobody thanks democracy for GPT-5, and nobody should thank the Five-Year Plan for robot backflips.**

把创新归功于政治制度是愚蠢的：没人感谢民主制度造就了GPT-5，也没必要感谢五年计划让机器人做后空翻。

---

**Last year's Gala, Unitree's robots did a wobbly folk dance twirling handkerchiefs.**

去年的春晚，宇树机器人跳了一段摇摇晃晃的转手绢民族舞。

**This year: swarm coordination at 4 m/s, drunken boxing, and aerial backflips while swinging weapons near children's heads.**

今年：4米/秒的集群协调、醉拳、在孩子们头顶附近挥舞武器的同时做空中后空翻。

**That kind of leap in twelve months doesn't come from genius alone.**

十二个月内的这种飞跃，不是靠天才就能实现的。

**It comes from the ability to fail cheaply and recover fast.**

它来自于低成本失败和快速恢复的能力。

---

**I've been in the U.S. robotics startup world.**

我在美国机器人创业圈待过。

**Let me tell you what that looks like on this side.**

让我告诉你这边是什么情况。

---

**I know a really good American research team that was doing aggressive full-body control experiments with Unitree robots about a year ago.**

我认识一个非常优秀的美国研究团队，大约一年前在用宇树机器人做激进的全身体控制实验。

**They fried three machines.**

他们烧坏了三台机器。

**Then they just... stopped.**

然后他们就……停下来了。

**Not out of ideas but out of patience.**

不是因为没想法，而是因为没耐心了。

**Getting replacements shipped across the Pacific, clearing customs, coordinating repairs as the iteration cycle went from days to weeks to "you know what, let's just not try that move anymore."**

等待替换件跨太平洋运输、过海关、协调维修，迭代周期从几天变成几周，最后变成"算了，那个动作不试了"。

---

**At the YC robotics startup where I worked, we tried making parts locally in the Bay Area.**

在我工作过的YC机器人创业公司，我们尝试在湾区本地制造零件。

**It was slower than shipping from China.**

这比从中国运过来还慢。

**Later we found some good U.S. suppliers eventually but scattered around the country, inconsistent quality, hard to count on.**

后来我们最终找到了一些好的美国供应商，但分散在全国各地，质量不稳定，难以依靠。

**A replacement actuator that a Hangzhou team gets by tomorrow morning? For us, that was a multi-week adventure.**

杭州团队明天早上就能拿到的替换执行器？对我们来说，那是好几周的冒险。

---

**That's not a talent gap.**

这不是人才差距。

**That's a logistics gap.**

这是物流差距。

**Chinese engineers and American engineers are both incredibly talented.**

中国工程师和美国工程师都非常有才华。

**I've worked with both, and the difference in raw ability is negligible.**

我和两者都合作过，原始能力的差别可以忽略不计。

**What's not negligible is how fast they can turn ideas into physical prototypes and iterate on them.**

不可忽略的是他们能把想法变成物理原型并迭代的速度。

---

**The Pearl River Delta around Shenzhen, plus the Yangtze River Delta around Shanghai and Hangzhou, where Unitree is actually based, these two clusters produce motors, sensors, actuators, custom PCBs at speeds that feel like cheating.**

深圳周围的珠江三角洲，加上上海和杭州周围的长江三角洲——宇树的实际所在地——这两个产业集群生产电机、传感器、执行器、定制PCB的速度快得像作弊。

**When Unitree wants to test a new joint design, they walk down the street.**

当宇树想测试一个新的关节设计时，他们走下楼到街上就行。

---

**That's why China shipped 90% of the ~13,000 humanoid robots sold globally last year.**

这就是为什么去年全球售出的约13,000台人形机器人中，90%来自中国。

**That's why Unitree's G1 costs $16,000 while Tesla's Optimus Gen2 will be over $20,000.**

这就是为什么宇树G1售价16,000美元，而特斯拉Optimus Gen2要超过20,000美元。

**That's why Unitree plans to ship 10,000–20,000 units this year.**

这就是为什么宇树计划今年出货10,000到20,000台。

---

**Not strategy.**

不是战略。

**Not policy.**

不是政策。

**Just two insanely dense manufacturing ecosystems doing what they do.**

只是两个极度密集的制造业生态系统在做它们擅长的事。

---

**Here's the other narrative I keep hearing: "China is winning because of unlimited state subsidies."**

这是我一直听到的另一个说法："中国赢了是因为无限的政府补贴。"。

**This is lazy thinking.**

这是懒惰的思考。

---

**Yes, Chinese robotics companies have raised from state-backed funds: Unitree's backers include CITIC, China Mobile, and others with government ties.**

是的，中国机器人公司从政府支持的基金融资：宇树的投资方包括中信、中国移动等有政府背景的企业。

**But let's be real: the U.S. is not poor.**

但说实话：美国并不穷。

**Silicon Valley has significantly more capital, much deeper pockets, and far more sophisticated investors than the Chinese venture ecosystem right now.**

硅谷目前拥有比中国风投生态系统多得多的资本、深得多的口袋、以及老练得多的投资者。

**American VCs have decades more experience pricing risk, backing high-variance bets, and giving founders room to fail fast.**

美国风投有几十年更多的风险定价、支持高方差赌注、给创始人快速失败空间的经验。

**If this were a pure spending contest, America would win handily.**

如果这是一场纯粹的花钱比赛，美国会轻松获胜。

---

**In fact, China's state capital comes with baggage.**

事实上，中国的国有资本是有包袱的。

**State money is classified as "state assets" (国有资产), and founders who take it face serious personal liability if things go south.**

国有资本被归类为"国有资产"，如果事情搞砸了，拿了钱的创始人要承担严重的个人责任。

**This pushes a lot of capital toward politically safe bets rather than the most innovative companies.**

这推动大量资本流向政治安全的赌注，而非最具创新性的公司。

---

**But here's the thing that actually matters: the difference isn't how much money you have, it's how far that money goes.**

但真正重要的是：差别不在于你有多少钱，而在于这些钱能走多远。

**In the Bay Area, a million dollars buys you a few months of runway and maybe one major hardware iteration.**

在湾区，一百万美元够你撑几个月，也许够一次重大硬件迭代。

**In Shenzhen, that same million buys you ten iterations.**

在深圳，同样的一百万够你迭代十次。

**Same engineers, same ideas but wildly different output.**

同样的工程师，同样的想法，但产出天差地别。

---

**The "state subsidy" argument is a coping mechanism.**

"政府补贴"的说法是一种应对机制。

**It's easier to say "they have unfair government money" than to admit "their ecosystem lets them do R&D 10x cheaper than we can."**

说"他们有不公平的政府资金"比承认"他们的生态系统让他们能以我们十分之一的成本做研发"更容易。

---

**So strip it all away: both sides have capital, both sides have talent.**

所以抛开一切：两边都有资本，两边都有人才。

**What's left?**

剩下的是什么？

**The supply chain.**

供应链。

---

**Step one: just accept it.**

第一步：接受现实。

**China's robotics industry is doing really well right now.**

中国的机器人产业现在做得真的很好。

**You don't need exotic explanations, not "state capitalism," not "they're copying," not "it's all hype."**

你不需要奇特的解释，不是"国家资本主义"，不是"他们在抄袭"，不是"都是炒作"。

**It's real progress, driven by real engineering, on top of real infrastructure.**

这是真正的进步，由真正的工程驱动，建立在真正的基础设施之上。

**The sooner the West stops looking for reasons to dismiss it, the sooner it can figure out how to compete.**

西方越早停止寻找理由来否定它，就能越早弄清楚如何竞争。

---

**Step two: think clearly about what competing actually means.**

第二步：清楚地思考竞争到底意味着什么。

**A friend who founded a U.S. humanoid startup recently tweeted that "nuking El Segundo and giving green cards to like 1000 of the right people would probably go a long way."**

一位创立美国人形机器人创业公司的朋友最近发推说："炸了埃尔塞贡多（特斯拉工厂所在地），给1000个合适的人发绿卡，可能会有很大帮助。"

**More "sharks in the water."**

更多"水中的鲨鱼"。

**I get the energy.**

我理解这种劲头。

**But sharks need water.**

但鲨鱼需要水。

---

**Drop 1,000 brilliant robotics engineers into the Bay Area tomorrow and they'll hit the exact same walls: multi-week waits for parts, robots they can't replace quickly, half their time wasted on logistics instead of actual research.**

明天往湾区丢1000个杰出的机器人工程师，他们会撞上同样的墙：等零件要等好几周，机器人坏了不能快速替换，一半时间浪费在物流上而不是真正的研究。

**This is America's problem to solve, and it has to be solved by building American infrastructure.**

这是美国要解决的问题，必须通过建设美国基础设施来解决。

**Foreign talent helps on the margin, but you can't graft a supply chain onto green cards.**

外国人才在边际上有帮助，但你不能把供应链嫁接在绿卡上。

---

**And this stuff takes time -- years of boring, unglamorous work.**

而且这需要时间——多年枯燥、不风光的工作。

**Building a network of local suppliers and figuring out how to make them work together.**

建立本地供应商网络，想办法让他们协同工作。

**Relentlessly driving down cost and delivery time the way Chinese manufacturers have been doing for decades.**

像中国制造商几十年来一直在做的那样，无情地降低成本和交付时间。

**None of it makes for good tweets or pitch decks.**

这些都不是好的推文或融资演讲稿的素材。

---

**The goal is dead simple to say and brutally hard to do: make it so a robotics lab in SF, Pittsburgh, or Boston can get a replacement part in two days, not two weeks.**

目标说起来简单得要死，做起来难得要命：让旧金山、匹兹堡或波士顿的机器人实验室能在两天内拿到替换零件，而不是两周。

**That's not a DARPA problem.**

这不是DARPA（国防高级研究计划局）的问题。

**Not an immigration problem.**

不是移民问题。

**It's an industrial base problem.**

这是工业基础问题。

---

**Until that changes, expect more viral robot videos from across the Pacific.**

在改变之前，期待更多来自太平洋彼岸的病毒式机器人视频吧。

**Not because China cracked some code or stole it from the US.**

不是因为中国破解了什么代码或从美国偷来的。

**Because they made it cheap and fast to break stuff and try again.**

而是因为他们让破坏东西和再次尝试变得便宜又快速。

**In engineering, that's most of the game.**

在工程领域，这就是大部分的游戏规则。
