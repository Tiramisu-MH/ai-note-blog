## 1. 工业化还能被“跳过”吗？

**EN**  
The idea that developing countries can bypass manufacturing and leapfrog straight to services has become more fashionable. Services are becoming a more important source of economic growth and commercial activity, and many question whether traditional industrialization still matters — or is even possible. India’s business process outsourcing boom, UAEs success with financial services, and the Phillippines’s call centre surge are all held up as proof that countries can potentially skip factory-based production.

**中**  
“发展中国家可以绕过制造业，直接跃迁到服务业”的观点越来越流行。服务业确实正在成为经济增长与商业活动中更重要的来源，于是很多人开始质疑：传统意义上的工业化是否仍然重要——甚至是否还可能实现。印度的业务流程外包繁荣、阿联酋在金融服务上的成功、以及菲律宾呼叫中心的增长，都常被当作证据：国家也许可以跳过工厂式生产。

---

**EN**  
We should acknowledge new service-based development opportunities. But the notion that countries can skip industrialization is mostly wishful thinking. The evidence shows that very few countries have developed without a strong, competitive manufacturing base. The reason is that services cannot replace what manufacturing uniquely provides: sustained productivity growth, innovation, trade, and the foundation for a strong economy.

**中**  
我们应该承认：确实出现了新的“服务业驱动发展”的机会。但“国家可以跳过工业化”的想法，多半是一厢情愿。证据表明：几乎没有国家能在缺少强大且有竞争力的制造业基础的情况下实现发展。原因在于，服务业无法替代制造业所独有地提供的东西：持续的生产率增长、创新、贸易能力，以及强健经济的底座。

---

## 2. 历史经验：富起来的国家几乎都走过工厂

**EN**  
Virtually every country that has successfully transformed from poor to rich has done so through industrialization. Between 1750 and 1950, the West’s establishment as the world’s economic hegemon was fundamentally a process of becoming the world’s manufacturing hegemon. Since 1950, this pattern has persisted with remarkable consistency.

**中**  
几乎所有成功实现“从穷到富”的国家，都是通过工业化完成转型的。1750 到 1950 年间，西方确立其全球经济霸权，本质上是其成为“全球制造业霸权”的过程。1950 年之后，这一模式仍以惊人的一致性持续存在。

---

**EN**  
A World Bank study published in 2008 identified 13 countries that sustained annual growth rates of 7% or higher for a period of 25 years or longer. Among these growth miracles, only two — Botswana and Oman, both small countries with highly idiosyncratic economic structures — achieved this without manufacturing-led development. Every other country on that list built its prosperity by expanding manufacturing capabilities.

**中**  
世界银行 2008 年的一项研究识别出 13 个国家：它们将年均增长率维持在 7% 或更高，并持续 25 年以上。在这些“增长奇迹”中，只有两个国家——博茨瓦纳与阿曼（都是经济结构非常特殊的小国）——不是依靠“制造业引领的发展”实现的。名单上的其他国家，都是通过扩张制造业能力来积累繁荣。

---

**EN**  
More recent data by the UN Industrial Development Organisation (UNIDO) arrive at similar conclusions. In their Industrial Development Report 2026, they highlight that 64% of growth episodes over the last 50 years can be directly attributed to manufacturing.

**中**  
联合国工业发展组织（UNIDO）的更近期数据也得出类似结论。在《工业发展报告 2026》中，他们指出：过去 50 年的增长阶段中，有 64% 可以直接归因于制造业。

---

**EN**  
Specific case-study evidence strengthens this hypothesis. The two most successful economic development stories of recent times as measured by sustained economic growth, China and Vietnam, achieved rapid growth through export-led industrialization.

**中**  
更具体的案例也加强了这一判断。以持续经济增长衡量，近几十年最成功的两个发展故事——中国与越南——都通过“出口导向的工业化”实现了快速增长。

---

**EN**  
In fact, the full historical record shows that no country except a few natural-resource rich states (mostly oil-dependent) or tiny financial havens has achieved high living standards without developing a competitive manufacturing sector. This is why “industrialized country” and “developed country” are used interchangeably.

**中**  
实际上，完整的历史记录显示：除少数自然资源极其丰富的国家（多为石油依赖型）或极小的金融避风港之外，没有国家能在不发展出有竞争力的制造业部门的情况下达到高生活水平。这也是为什么“工业化国家”和“发达国家”常常被当作同义词使用。

---

## 3. 反证：过早去工业化会拖垮增长

**EN**  
The flip side tells the same story. Regions that have experienced premature de-industrialization have suffered growth slowdowns. Latin America, which went through severe de-industrialization in the 1980s and 1990s, saw manufacturing’s declining share of GDP coincide with decelerated growth. In Africa, premature de-industrialization caused negative economic growth in the same time period. Even OECD countries have experienced growth slowdowns alongside deindustrialization in the past few decades.

**中**  
反过来看，故事同样成立：经历“过早去工业化”的地区往往出现增长放缓。拉丁美洲在 1980、1990 年代经历严重去工业化，制造业在 GDP 中占比下降与增长减速同时发生。非洲在同一时期的过早去工业化甚至导致了负增长。甚至在过去几十年里，经合组织（OECD）国家也在去工业化的同时出现了增长放缓。

---

## 4. 为什么制造业更能带来生产率与创新？

**EN**  
The new appeal of services is understandable, and we shouldn’t discount them. Many digital services do indeed provide some innovation and development. Megacities in India, such as Bangalore and Hyderabad, are prime examples. But when we examine what fundamentally drives productivity growth and innovation, manufacturing retains fundamental advantages that services struggle to match.

**中**  
服务业之所以重新受到追捧是可以理解的，我们也不应低估它。许多数字服务确实带来创新与发展；印度的班加罗尔、海得拉巴等超级城市就是典型例子。但当我们追问“生产率增长与创新到底由什么驱动”时，制造业仍然拥有服务业难以匹敌的根本优势。

---

**EN**  
Manufacturing activities lend themselves more easily to mechanization and chemical processing. This, combined with the ease of spatially concentrating manufacturing production, enhances the potential of productivity growth through economies of scale — both static based on output level and dynamic through learning-by-doing effects. These advantages are difficult for services to replicate.

**中**  
制造业活动更容易实现机械化与化学过程化；再加上制造业生产更容易在空间上集聚，使其能通过规模经济显著提升生产率增长潜力——既包括产出规模带来的“静态规模经济”，也包括“干中学”带来的“动态规模效应”。这些优势很难被服务业复制。

---

**EN**  
Additionally, studies using global input-output data find that manufacturing generates significantly higher productivity spillovers to other sectors than services do. Manufacturing also demonstrates stronger backward and forward linkages — purchasing inputs from and selling outputs to a wider range of sectors — which amplifies its impact on the broader economy.

**中**  
此外，基于全球投入产出数据的研究发现：制造业向其他部门带来的生产率外溢显著高于服务业。制造业也表现出更强的前向与后向关联——从更广泛的部门采购投入、并向更广泛的部门出售产出——从而放大其对整体经济的影响。

---

**EN**  
The innovation dimension is perhaps the most crucial one. Manufacturing firms spend heavily on research and development (R&D), generating strong innovation spillovers throughout the economy. In fact, manufacturing is attributed to 53% of global R&D activity. Manufacturing provides the material foundation for innovation, creates demand for new technologies, and enables the accumulation of productive capabilities that underpin further innovation.

**中**  
创新维度也许是最关键的。制造业企业在研发（R&D）上投入巨大，进而在全经济范围内形成强烈的创新外溢。事实上，全球 53% 的研发活动与制造业有关。制造业为创新提供物质基础，创造对新技术的需求，并使得“生产能力”的积累成为可能——而这些能力又会支撑进一步创新。

---

## 5. 贸易：服务业能否替代制造业出口？

**EN**  
One reason the “services revolution” narrative has gained traction is that trade in services has expanded. Thanks to digitalization and the internet, many services can now be delivered remotely.

**中**  
“服务业革命”叙事之所以更受欢迎，一个原因是服务贸易确实在扩张。数字化与互联网让许多服务可以远程交付。

---

**EN**  
But when we examine the evidence carefully, manufacturing remains dominant in international trade — and this matters enormously for economic development. Export of goods (dominated by manufactured goods) accounts for roughly 80% of all global exports.

**中**  
但如果仔细看数据，制造业仍然主导国际贸易——这对经济发展至关重要。货物出口（以制造品为主）约占全球出口总额的 80%。

---

**EN**  
Manufacturing goods are inherently more storable, standardizable, and transportable than most services. ... No one has yet found a way to remotely cut someone’s hair, clean someone’s house, serve someone’s food, build someone’s house, or mow someone’s lawn.

**中**  
相较于大多数服务，制造品天然更易储存、更标准化、也更可运输……至今还没有人发明一种方式，能远程给人理发、打扫房子、端盘子、盖房子或修剪草坪。

---

## 6. 自动化会堵死“制造业致富”这条路吗？

**EN**  
Perhaps the most common objection to manufacturing-led development today is that automation will eliminate the jobs that made industrialization so powerful for development in the past.

**中**  
当下对“制造业引领发展”最常见的反对意见之一是：自动化会消灭过去让工业化如此强大的那些工作岗位。

---

**EN**  
This fear is understandable but overstated. ... More recently, automation in manufacturing has generally not led to net job losses.

**中**  
这种担忧可以理解，但被夸大了……更近期的情况是：制造业自动化通常并未导致净就业减少。

---

**EN**  
For developing countries, multiple barriers limit automation’s impact. Most automation technologies are developed for high-wage contexts and only become economically viable when labor costs are high.

**中**  
对发展中国家而言，多重障碍会限制自动化的影响。大多数自动化技术是在高工资环境下发展起来的，只有当劳动力成本高时才更具有经济可行性。

---

## 7. 中国案例：机器人多，不等于制造业不能吸纳就业

**EN**  
China’s manufacturing ecosystem currently employs roughly 200 million workers — more than a quarter of the national workforce — demonstrating an exceptional capacity for labour absorption.

**中**  
中国的制造业生态系统目前大约雇佣 2 亿工人——超过全国劳动力的四分之一——展现出极强的就业吸纳能力。

---

**EN**  
China’s installed stock of industrial robots grew ten-fold from 2014 to 2024 — from approximately 200,000 to 2,000,0000.

**中**  
2014 到 2024 年，中国工业机器人保有量增长了十倍——从约 20 万台增长到约 200 万台（原文数字写作 2,000,0000）。

## 8. 中国制造占比高，会“挤出”其他国家工业化机会吗？

**EN**  
The claim that China’s manufacturing dominance is preventing industrialization elsewhere overlooks a crucial fact: very few countries managed to industrialize successfully even before China’s manufacturing boom.

**中**  
“由于中国制造业主导，其他国家无法工业化”的说法忽略了一个关键事实：即便在中国制造业大爆发之前，也几乎没有多少国家能成功实现工业化。

---

**EN**  
More tellingly, Vietnam ... is heavily integrated into China’s manufacturing ecosystem.

**中**  
更能说明问题的是：越南——人均制造品出口增长最亮眼的国家——与中国制造业生态高度融合。

---

## 9. 结论：通往繁荣的路仍然要“做东西”

**EN**  
Most successful development stories ... have run through the factory floor. ... The path to prosperity still runs through making things.

**中**  
大多数成功的发展故事……都发生在工厂车间之上。……通往繁荣的道路，仍然要靠“做东西”。
